Explanation:

Trend is the short-term average market price compared to the long-term average market price of a trading pair. Specifically, it’s the 14-day WMA divided by the 60-day WMA. A value above 1 indicates an uptrend, and below 1 indicates a downtrend.

RSI is the Relative Strength Index of a trading pair. A value of 50 is neutral with above a uptrend, and below a downtrend.

The Combined Trend is the average Trend of all tickers plus normalised average RSI: (avgTrend+((avgRSI/100)+0.5))/2. This shows the general trend of the index, where above 1.1 and below 0.9 indicates extreme trends. Downturn protection is based on this indicator.

Beta is the relative volatility of a trading pair compared to an index. In this case, the index is the grouping (ZAR, USDC, USDT). A low or negative beta suggests an unrelated or inverse correlation with the broader index, 1 indicates the volatility matches the average and higher is more volatile.

And lastly ATR% is the Average True Range divided by the 60-day WMA. This gives the average daily movement of a ticker compared to its price.
